## 🏔️ The Map: Underwater to Floating Cities

Hidden Mountain abandons the traditional flat-MMO map design. The new zone is **vertically stacked** across five distinct elevation layers:

| Layer | Altitude | Content | Theme |
|-------|:---:|------|-------|
| **Abyssal Trench (深渊沟壑)** | Sea level –500m | Underwater ruins, submerged Mohist workshops, aquatic traversal puzzles | Bioluminescent darkness |
| **Hui Valley (晦谷)** | Ground level | Mohist industrial heartland, forge districts, crafting hubs | Smoke, gears, industry |
| **Tianxing Plateau (天陉)** | Mountain slope | Residential quarters, sect shrines, market towns | Misty peaks, pine forests |
| **Fuyao Peak (扶摇峰)** | Summit | Mohist grand temple, story climax, clockwork colossus boss | Wind-swept, sacred |
| **Sky Cities (浮空城)** | Above clouds | Endgame floating platforms, aerial traversal, hidden sanctuaries | Sunrise-lit, ethereal |

### Traversal Mechanics

Moving between layers isn't just climbing — each transition uses a different mechanic:

```
Abyssal → Hui Valley:    Underwater thermal vents (ride updrafts)
Hui Valley → Tianxing:   Mohist mechanical elevators (puzzle-locked)
Tianxing → Fuyao Peak:   Wind gliders + cliff climbing (endurance test)
Fuyao Peak → Sky Cities: Gravity manipulation devices (Mohist tech)
```

The development team emphasized **"风、光、石"** (wind, light, stone) as the three design pillars. Expect environmental puzzles that require manipulating all three elements simultaneously.

## 🔧 DIY Mechanical Crafting System

This is the headline gameplay system of v2.0 — and potentially the most ambitious feature Where Winds Meet has ever added.

### What You Can Build

| Category | Examples | Combat Use |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Combat Contraptions** | Spring-loaded fist extenders, flame-spray gauntlets, grappling arms | Augment Gauntlets combos |
| **Vehicles** | Pedal-powered gliders, steam carts, mechanical horses | Zone traversal, mounted combat |
| **Utility Gadgets** | Auto-miners, resource detectors, self-playing instruments | Resource gathering, puzzle solving |
| **Decorative Machines** | Clockwork pets, animated homestead decorations, music boxes | Homestead customization |

### Crafting Economy

```
Gather Mohist Materials → Process at Forge → Assemble Contraption → Test & Iterate
         ↓                        ↓                    ↓
    New resource nodes       Player-driven       Blueprints found
    in all 5 layers         crafting stations    via exploration
```

The crafting system is **not instanced** — contraptions have durability and can be traded on the player market. This creates a new economic layer on top of existing gear trading.

## FAQ

<div class="faq-section">

### Q: Is Hidden Mountain really free?

**Yes.** Like all Where Winds Meet expansions, Hidden Mountain v2.0 is free for all players. Cosmetic items, the Lingyin Pool gacha, and battle passes are paid, but the entire map, story, Gauntlets weapon, crafting system, and bosses are free.

### Q: Do I need to start a new character?

**No.** Hidden Mountain is a continuation of your existing character's journey. However, the new multi-character system (introduced in v1.8) lets you create an alt specifically for the Mohist sect if you prefer.

### Q: Will old zones be updated too?

Yes. The visual overhaul (textures, lighting, dynamic wind) applies game-wide, not just to Hidden Mountain. Older zones like Qinghe and Kaifeng will look noticeably better.

### Q: Is the Mohist Sect required for Gauntlets?

No — Gauntlets work with any sect. But Mohist sect passives provide Gauntlets-specific bonuses, and some crafting recipes are sect-exclusive.

### Q: How big is the download?

Exact size TBA. The developers have stated they're working on reducing total install size despite the new content. Expect a large patch but potentially a smaller total footprint than previous versions due to asset optimization.
