## 🥇 T0 Builds — The Best of the Best

### Bellstrike-Umbra (Strategic Sword + Heaven Quake Spear) — **NEW #1**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Weapons** | Strategic Sword (main) + Heaven Quake Spear (off-hand) |
| **Playstyle** | Bleed stacking → DoT settlement → detonation burst |
| **Best In** | **PvE (raids, bosses, Martial Domain), PvP, Arena** — all three modes |
| **Skill Ceiling** | Medium-High (requires timing detonations) |
| **F2P Friendly** | Moderate (Strategic Sword accessible; Heaven Quake Spear needs investment) |

**What changed:** The May 28 "Path Balance" patch granted Bellstrike-Umbra an **18% Affinity damage boost on DoT settlement**. This single change catapulted it from a strong T1 contender to the undisputed #1 build. The core loop — stacking multiple Bleed layers then detonating them with Heaven Quake Spear finishers — now hits harder than anything else in the game.

**Why it dominates:**
- **Bleed stacking scales multiplicatively** with the new 18% Affinity bonus.
- Heaven Quake Spear's eruption mechanic converts all active DoT stacks into instant burst damage.
- Works in all content — no mode-specific weaknesses.
- Both weapons benefit from the same stat priority (Attack Power > Crit > Affinity).

**Rotation priority (PvE):**
```
1. Apply 3× Bleed with Strategic Sword combo
2. Weave in Heaven Quake Spear opener for Affinity mark
3. Settle DoTs when all 3 stacks + mark are active
4. Detonate with Heaven Quake Spear finisher (massive burst window)
5. Repeat
```

**Key stats to stack:** Attack Power > Affinity Damage > Critical Rate > Critical Damage

### Bamboocut-Dust (Vernal Umbrella + Mortal Rope Dart)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Weapons** | Vernal Umbrella (main) + Mortal Rope Dart (off-hand) |
| **Playstyle** | Ranged sustained DPS, AoE room-clearing, raid boss specialist |
| **Best In** | **PvE** (raids, Martial Domain boss floors, world bosses) |
| **Skill Ceiling** | Low-Medium (forgiving range, simple rotation) |
| **F2P Friendly** | High (both weapons farmable early; low gear threshold) |

**Status:** Remains T0 for ranged raid DPS — no other ranged setup matches its sustained output against large hitbox bosses. However, it no longer rules alone. Bellstrike-Umbra's new ceiling means Bamboocut-Dust is now the **specialist T0** rather than the universal best pick.

**When to pick Bamboocut-Dust over Bellstrike-Umbra:**
- You need safe ranged positioning (world bosses with melee AoE).
- Martial Domain floors with Mist effects that punish melee uptime (Thorn Armor, Vitality Drain).
- Multi-target fights where Rope Dart's AoE spreads outperform single-target burst.
- You are on mobile or have higher ping — the rotation is far more forgiving.

**Key stats to stack:** Attack Power > Ranged Damage Bonus > Critical Rate > Attack Speed

## 🥈 T1 Builds — Strong, Viable, Situationally Excellent

### Mo Blade (Thundercry Blade + Stormbreaker Spear) — **PROMOTED T2 ⟶ T1**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Weapons** | Thundercry Blade (main) + Stormbreaker Spear (off-hand) |
| **Playstyle** | Bruiser-tank hybrid, sustained melee damage with high survivability |
| **Best In** | **PvE** (solo content, boss tanking), **Arena** (durability + damage) |
| **Skill Ceiling** | Medium |
| **F2P Friendly** | High (Thundercry Blade is a starter weapon; Stormbreaker farmable) |

**What changed — two major buffs:**
1. **Physical Attack cap doubled:** from 60 ⟶ 120. Mo Blade can now stack significantly more raw damage than before.
2. **Stonebreaker combo damage +30%.** The signature Stormbreaker Spear follow-up now hits like a truck, closing the burst gap that previously held Mo Blade in T2.

**Why it matters:** Mo Blade was always durable — now it has the damage to match. It is the best solo-content build in the game and a strong off-tank option for groups that want one flexible slot instead of a dedicated tank.

**Ideal use cases:**
- Solo boss farming / open-world elite hunting.
- Martial Domain as the "bruiser" flex slot (tank + DPS hybrid).
- Arena matches where you want to outlast opponents rather than burst them down.

### Dual Blades (Infernal Twinblades) — **DEMOTED T0 ⟶ T1 (PvE)**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Weapons** | Infernal Twinblades (dual-wield, single weapon type) |
| **Playstyle** | Fast-paced melee DPS, high APM, burst windows |
| **Best In** | **Arena (still T0)**, **PvE** (T1 now) |
| **Skill Ceiling** | High (requires tight execution and animation cancelling) |
| **F2P Friendly** | Moderate (high gear dependency to reach competitive DPS) |

**What changed:** Dual Blades drops from T0 to T1 in PvE — but holds T0 in Arena. The v1.8 "Depleting Defense" mechanic reworked how stamina reduction functions in PvP, capping it at **40%**. This actually benefits Dual Blades in Arena, where its pressure-heavy playstyle thrives under the new cap.

In PvE, the issue is simpler: Bellstrike-Umbra and Bamboocut-Dust simply outscale it now. Dual Blades remains excellent — top-tier even — but it no longer competes for the #1 PvE slot.

**When to still pick Dual Blades:**
- **Arena PvP** — still the best 1v1 pressure build in the game.
- Content where you value mobility and i-frames over raw damage ceiling.
- You enjoy high-APM gameplay and are willing to invest time mastering it.

### Healer — Silkbind-Deluge (Soulshade Umbrella + Panacea Fan) — **PROMOTED T2 ⟶ T1**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Weapons** | Soulshade Umbrella (main) + Panacea Fan (off-hand) |
| **Playstyle** | Healer-support with genuine damage contribution |
| **Best In** | **Group PvE** (raids, Martial Domain), **Arena** (sustain comps) |
| **Skill Ceiling** | Medium (managing heal uptime + weaving damage) |
| **F2P Friendly** | Very High (always in demand; low gear threshold to be effective) |

**What changed — two transformative buffs:**
1. **Floating Grace now grants a 24% damage boost.** This is huge — the healer can now buff the entire party's damage output while healing, making Silkbind-Deluge a **damage multiplier for the group**, not just a health-bar filler.
2. **Restoring Blossom durations doubled.** HoT (Heal-over-Time) windows are now long enough that healers have meaningful windows to cast damage skills between refreshes.

**Why this matters:** Before this patch, healers were a "necessary slot" — you brought one because you had to. Now, a well-played Silkbind-Deluge adds measurable DPS to the group through Floating Grace uptime, making it a genuine **force multiplier**. Parties running a T1 healer clear content faster than parties without one — even accounting for the lost DPS slot.

**Rotation priority (group PvE):**
```
1. Maintain Restoring Blossom on tank (doubled duration = less recasting)
2. Apply Floating Grace before DPS burst windows (24% damage boost)
3. Weave Panacea Fan offensive skills during HoT windows
4. Emergency heal only when needed — proactive HoTs are the priority
```

## ⚔️ Arena-Specific Meta (v1.8 "Depleting Defense")

The v1.8 Arena rework introduced the **Depleting Defense** mechanic: stamina reduction from pressure and guard breaks is now **capped at 40%**. This single change reshuffled the Arena tier list.

| Arena Tier | Builds |
|------------|--------|
| **T0** | **Bellstrike-Umbra**, **Dual Blades** |
| **T1** | Bamboocut-Dust, Mo Blade, Healer (Silkbind-Deluge) |
| **T2** | Stonesplit-Might, Stonesplit-Strength, Phantom Weave, Frost Clad, Gale Dash |

**Key takeaways:**
- **Bellstrike-Umbra and Dual Blades** are the only two S-tier Arena builds. Dual Blades retains T0 in Arena thanks to pressure uptime; Bellstrike-Umbra's burst counters the stamina cap by killing opponents before attrition matters.
- **Tank builds (Stonesplit)** suffer the most from Depleting Defense — their attrition-based strategy is less effective when stamina drain is capped.
- **Healer** jumps to T1 in Arena: sustained healing comps benefit from the slower pace that the stamina cap creates.

## 🎯 What This Means For You

### If you are a DPS main:
**Your new goal is Bellstrike-Umbra.** Bamboocut-Dust remains excellent and is the safer, easier pick — especially if you play on mobile or prefer ranged. But if you want the highest ceiling in the game, start investing in Strategic Sword and Heaven Quake Spear. The 18% Affinity boost on DoT settlement is unlikely to be nerfed soon (the devs have stated they are monitoring it but are satisfied with the current balance), so this build should dominate for the remainder of Season 3.

### If you are a tank or healer:
**Your value has never been higher.** Mo Blade's promotion to T1 and Healer's transformation into a damage-buffing force multiplier mean that support roles are no longer just "necessary slots" — they actively speed up clears. Group leaders are actively seeking competent Mo Blade and Silkbind-Deluge players.

### If you play Arena:
**Learn the Depleting Defense rhythm.** The 40% stamina reduction cap changes how pressure works — burst builds (Bellstrike-Umbra, Dual Blades) are king. If you main a tank, consider adding a DPS off-spec for Arena. The days of winning by attrition alone are over.

### If you play an off-meta build:
**Keep playing it.** One of the standout features of Where Winds Meet Season 3 is that **all 11 weapon types are viable**. The tier list measures optimization, not playability. Player skill remains the single largest differentiator in this game — a master of Gale Dash or Frost Clad will consistently beat an average Bellstrike-Umbra player. The gap between T0 and T2 in Where Winds Meet is narrower than in most comparable action MMOs.
