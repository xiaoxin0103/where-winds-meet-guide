## Mortal Rope Dart: Core Mechanics

The Rope Dart is a **hybrid weapon** — melee slashes at close range, grappling-hook pulls at mid-range, and a unique summoning mechanic that sets it apart from every other weapon in the game.

### The Pull Mechanic

Your most defining tool. The Rope Dart can **hook and pull** enemies toward you, or **pull yourself** toward enemies. This is what makes you the best engage tool in group PvP and invaluable for controlling spread-out mob packs.

| Action | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Hook an enemy | Pull them into melee range → immediate combo follow-up |
| Hook terrain | Gap-close across the battlefield |
| Hook a downed ally | Pull them to safety for revive |

### Rat Summons

Unique to Mortal Rope Dart: certain skills summon **rodent allies** that fight alongside you:

| Rat Type | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Scout Rat** | Reveals nearby enemies, lootable objects, and trap locations |
| **Combat Rat** | Attacks your target, applies Poison DoT |
| **Bomb Rat** | Charges at enemy and explodes for AoE Fire damage |

> Rats scale with your **Affinity** stat. At high Affinity, a fully summoned rat swarm can contribute 10-15% of your total DPS.

### Bamboocut – Wind Core Buffs

| Buff | From | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Soul Break** | Rope Dart skills | Target takes +10% damage from you, +5% from allies (raid-wide debuff) |
| **Windstep** | Successful pull | +20% movement speed for 5s |
| **Rat King** | 3+ active rats | All rats deal +25% damage |
| **Pickpocket** | Disguise attacks | Hitting a disguised enemy steals 5% of their Qi as gold |

## Skill Breakdown

### Serpent's Grasp (Q)
- Launches the rope dart 15m forward
- **Hits enemy** → pulls them to you + 0.5s stun
- **Hits terrain** → pulls you to the impact point
- Generates 1 **Rat Token** on hit
- This is your opener. Always. Learn the max range.

### Rodent Swarm (Perception → Q)
- Costs 3 Rat Tokens
- Summons **2 Combat Rats + 1 Bomb Rat**
- Rats persist for 20 seconds
- Use when you have tokens banked and a fight is starting

### Whiplash Flurry (Light Attack Chain)
- 5-hit melee combo: slash → slash → whip → pull-in → spin
- Hits 3-5 apply **Soul Break** debuff
- The full chain takes ~2.8s — cancel after hit 4 if you need to dodge

### Hangman's Drop (Charged Heavy)
- Leap into the air, slam rope dart downward
- If target is debuffed with Soul Break: **+40% damage**
- Knocks down non-boss enemies
- Use after Whiplash Flurry for maximum burst

### Rat Bombardment (~)
- Command all active rats to focus-fire your target
- Bomb Rats explode immediately
- Combat Rats gain +30% attack speed for 8s
- 45s cooldown — save for priority targets

### Sneak Thief (Passive)
- While disguised, your first attack out-of-disguise deals **+50% damage**
- Works with the sect's disguise system
- Also triggers on Perception Forest bush ambushes

### Divine Counter Variant: Ensnare
- Block → immediate rope dart snare
- Roots the attacker in place for 2s
- Unlike other sects' damage counters, this is a **control counter** — use it to set up your team

## Rotation Guide

### PvE: Mob Packs

```
Serpent's Grasp (pull priority target) → Whiplash Flurry (apply Soul Break)
→ Hangman's Drop (burst) → Rodent Swarm → Rat Bombardment
```

1. Pull the ranged/caster enemy into the pack
2. Whiplash Flurry hits everything in melee range → Soul Break on multiple targets
3. Hangman's Drop the debuffed priority target
4. Drop rats to clean up

### PvE: Bosses

```
Open with Serpent's Grasp → Whiplash Flurry (full chain for Soul Break)
→ Rodent Swarm → Rat Bombardment → Swap to Twinblades
→ Berserk → Twinblade combo under Soul Break window
```

1. Soul Break gives +10% personal damage and **+5% raid-wide** — apply FIRST
2. Summon rats before Rat Bombardment for max burst
3. Swap to Twinblades while boss is debuffed
4. **Re-apply Soul Break** every 15s (debuff duration)

### PvP: Engage Combo

```
Serpent's Grasp (pull enemy healer/ranged) → Ensnare (block counter root)
→ Whiplash Flurry → Hangman's Drop → Swap to Twinblades → Berserk
```

1. **You are the engage.** Your job is to pull the backline into your team.
2. Once pulled + rooted, your team collapses on them.
3. If you miss the pull: **back off** and wait for cooldown. Don't fight without the engage.

### Pro Tips

- **Pull terrain for escapes** — Serpent's Grasp on a rooftop = instant vertical escape
- **Bomb Rats break Qi shields** — time Rat Bombardment for boss Exhaustion phase
- **Disguise before dungeon bosses** — Sneak Thief's +50% first-hit bonus applies in instances too
- **Rat tokens persist between fights** — pre-summon rats before engaging a boss
- **The Soul Break raid debuff is your real job** — your personal DPS is secondary to the +5% party-wide damage

## FAQ

**Q: Is Nine Mortal Ways good for solo players?**
A: Yes — but for a different reason than Well of Heaven. You won't face-tank bosses, but your rats give you a personal army, and the disguise system lets you bypass a lot of open-world combat entirely.

**Q: Do I really lose 10 Virtue every time a con fails?**
A: Yes. But "failure" means getting caught and jailed. Most cons are low-risk if you're patient. The key is knowing when to walk away.

**Q: What's the hardest thing about Mortal Rope Dart?**
A: **Pull accuracy.** If you miss Serpent's Grasp in PvP, you're useless for the next 12 seconds. Practice in Perception Forest until your hit rate is above 80%.

**Q: Can I skip the social/disguise stuff and just fight?**
A: You can, but you're missing half the sect's value. At minimum, learn the disguise mechanics — Sneak Thief's +50% damage opener is a huge combat buff.

**Q: How does the Double Rat build compare to pure Infernal Twinblades (Midnight Blades)?**
A: Double Rat has lower personal DPS but adds the Soul Break raid buff (+5% party damage). In solo play, Midnight Blades wins. In group content, Double Rat is better.

**Q: What happens if I fail too many cons and my Virtue tanks?**
A: Bounty hunters spawn in the open world. If they catch you, you go to jail (lose time + more Virtue). At very low Virtue, NPC vendors charge you more. You can recover Virtue by doing good deeds, but it's slow. **Don't let it spiral.**

**Q: Is the Unfettered Rope Dart worth the grind?**
A: Yes — Unfettered Rope Dart (Bamboocut – Dust) is the AoE Control variant. Wider pulls, longer stuns, and it can hook **multiple enemies** at once. Essential for high-level Perception Forest.
