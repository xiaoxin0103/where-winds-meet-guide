## How to Unlock the Soulshade Umbrella

You have **two options** — join the sect or steal the art.

### Method 1: Join Hollow Vale (Recommended)

1. Reach **Level 10** and access **Kaifeng** (complete Chapter 1)
2. Travel to **Eastern Kaifeng → Mistveil Forest** area
3. Find the Hollow Vale enclave and speak to the Sect Master
4. Complete the initiation trial — typically a healing challenge or escort quest
5. Pledge allegiance → Soulshade Umbrella unlocked immediately

### Method 2: Skill Theft (Sectless / Multi-Weapon)

1. Complete **Chapter 1: Heaven Has No Pier** to unlock Kaifeng
2. Unlock the **Touch of Death** Mystic Art (from *The Realm Endures* side quest)
3. Travel to **Soulshade Umbrella – Post Station** in eastern Kaifeng
4. Use Touch of Death to silently eliminate guards
5. Use **Skill Theft** on the Post Station master
6. Training costs are **higher** than joining, and you miss sect cosmetics

> 💡 **Recommendation:** Join the sect first to learn the weapon cheaply. You keep the martial art even after leaving. Many endgame healers join Hollow Vale, fully train Soulshade Umbrella, then switch to Silver Needle for Panacea Fan access and train both.

## 🏆 Best Build: "Healing Rain" (Soulshade Umbrella + Panacea Fan)

This is the premier **Silkbind – Deluge** healer/support build — the strongest healing setup in the game, essential for Martial Domain, World Bosses, and GvG.

### Weapon Pairing

| Slot | Weapon | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| **Primary** | Soulshade Umbrella | Passive healing turret, Dew generator, Exhausted debuffer |
| **Secondary** | Panacea Fan | Direct heals, burst healing, resurrection, Dew spender |

> You'll spend ~60% of combat on Fan and ~40% on Umbrella (deploy turret → swap to Fan).

### Core Healing Rotation

```
1. Soulshade Umbrella: Floating Grace (Q)
   → Team damage buff + initial heal
   
2. Soulshade Umbrella: Echoes of a Thousand Plants (~)
   → Deploy healing turret + generate Dew passively
   
3. Tab → Swap to Panacea Fan
   
4. Panacea Fan: Cloudburst Healing
   → AoE heal zone + instant 20 Dew
   
5. Panacea Fan: Heavy Attacks
   → Ranged single-target heals, Dew recovery
   
6. Panacea Fan: Light Dust After Morning Rain (50 Dew)
   → Strong HoT on self + lowest-HP ally
   → ALSO resurrects fallen allies!
   
7. When Umbrella expires (~15s) → Tab back → repeat from Step 1
```

### Dew Management — The Key Skill

**Dew** is your healing resource (max varies by build, typically ~100-150). Master this or fail:

| Action | Dew Change |
|--------|-----------|
| Echoes of a Thousand Plants (Umbrella Special) | +Generates over time (up to 60) |
| Umbrella Conversion (Tab swap) | +Generates (up to 30) |
| Cloudburst Healing (Fan) | +20 instantly |
| Fan Heavy Attacks | +Small amounts per hit |
| Light Dust After Morning Rain (Fan) | −50 Dew |
| Standing in Cloudburst zone | +Passive generation |

> ⚠️ **Golden Rule:** Always reserve at least 50 Dew for emergency *Light Dust* resurrection. A dead DPS does zero damage. A dead tank wipes the party.

| **Morale Chant** | Attacking or healing grants *Yi River* stack: +2 Physical Penetration + 1% damage/healing (stacks to 5, 12s) | ⬆️ Recommended |

### Why NOT Esoteric Revival?

*Esoteric Revival* (+50% healing on revived allies) looks tempting but is a **trap**:

- It only triggers when someone is **dying** — if that's happening often, your healing uptime is the problem, not the revival strength.
- **Morale Chant** provides consistent, always-active value instead of a conditional emergency button.
- In well-executed runs, nobody should hit 0 HP at all.

### PvP Alternatives

| Inner Art | Use Case |
|-----------|----------|
| **Fury Harvest** | Extra Vitality for Mystic Art spam |
| **Divine Roulette** | Buffs on successful deflects (dueling) |
| **Vital Leech** | Lifesteal from executions (Perception Forest) |

## Sect Rules & Daily Loop

Hollow Vale's rules reflect the life-death balance theme:

| Rule | Requirement | Tip |
|------|-------------|-----|
| **Cycle of Renewal** | Heal a certain amount of HP on other players weekly | Queue for co-op bosses — fast and reliable |
| **Blight Cleansing** | Defeat poisoned/undead enemies in the open world | Farm Mistveil Forest for easy clears |
| **Herbal Gathering** | Collect herbs weekly (sect shop sells gathering tools) | Do while waiting for co-op queues |
| **Balance Maintained** | Don't let your poison DoT kill-steal from sect members | Avoid AoE poison near low-HP sect allies |

> Compared to sects like Midnight Blades (constant PvP) or Velvet Shade (complex social systems), Hollow Vale's rules are relatively chill. Most are completed passively through normal healer gameplay.

## Advanced Tips

### 1. Pre-cast Before Pulls
Deploy *Echoes of a Thousand Plants* **before** the tank pulls. The umbrella persists and starts healing immediately — you'll have Dew built up before damage even starts.

### 2. Umbrella Positioning
The umbrella turret heals in a radius around where you cast it. In boss fights, place it near the tank and melee DPS stack. In spread fights, place it centrally.

### 3. Don't Overheal
If the party is at full HP, use Fan Heavy Attacks (ranged damage) or swap to Umbrella for *Pale Petal* Exhausted extension. Overhealing wastes Dew.

### 4. Resurrection Priority
*Light Dust After Morning Rain* resurrects: Tank > Healer (other) > Top DPS > Everyone else. A resurrected tank at 20% HP with umbrella healing is better than a full-HP DPS with no frontliner.

### 5. Martial Domain Specific
Bring **Flute of the Tides** and **Ivorybloom 4pc**. On timed kill floors, deploy umbrella → swap Fan → contribute damage while passive healing sustains the party. On boss floors, prioritize healing uptime over your own DPS.

### 6. GvG (Guild vs. Guild)
You are the #1 priority target for enemy assassins. Stay with your tank at all times. *Eaglerise 4pc* is non-negotiable — *Eagle Guard* will save your life repeatedly.

## Hybrid DPS Build (Solo / Small Group)

For solo content where pure healing is overkill, pair Soulshade Umbrella with a DPS weapon:

| Pairing | Playstyle |
|---------|-----------|
| **Soulshade Umbrella + Infernal Twinblades** | Deploy turret → swap Twinblades → aggressive melee DPS with passive self-sustain |
| **Soulshade Umbrella + Everspring Umbrella** | Double-umbrella: one heals, one controls. All-ranged, high survivability |
| **Soulshade Umbrella + Strategic Sword** | Turret healing + precision dueling. Good for Perception Forest solo runs |

Rotation: Cast *Echoes* + *Floating Grace* at fight start → swap to DPS weapon → fight normally. Umbrella sustains you in the background. Refresh when it expires.

> ⚠️ This is for overworld and solo instances only. In raids, GvG, and Martial Domain: **play pure healer or don't play Hollow Vale.**

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to join Hollow Vale to be a good healer?**
A: Not strictly — you can Skill Theft the Soulshade Umbrella and stay sectless or join Silver Needle for Panacea Fan. But joining gives cheaper training, sect cosmetics, and the Everspring Umbrella (AoE control variant).

**Q: Is Hollow Vale viable for solo players?**
A: Yes, with the hybrid DPS build. But it's slower than dedicated DPS sects. If you play 90% solo, consider Well of Heaven or Nine Mortal Ways instead.

**Q: Soulshade Umbrella vs Panacea Fan — which is better?**
A: They do different things. Umbrella = passive/background healing (fire-and-forget). Fan = active/direct healing (aimed, stronger). The meta build uses **both together**. If you can only have one, Umbrella is more versatile.

**Q: What's the hardest content for Hollow Vale healers?**
A: Martial Domain Hard Mode floors 15-20. Mist Effects like Thorn Armor (30% reflect) and Enrage Timer punish any healing mistakes. Bring your best gear and coordinate voice comms.

**Q: When will the Hollow Vale sect questline be fully unlocked?**
A: The sect is currently marked as "Locked" for its full questline, but the Soulshade Umbrella weapon and core Silkbind – Deluge path are fully playable. The full story unlock is expected in a future patch.
